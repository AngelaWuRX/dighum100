## Research Question

How do patterns of investment spending in the United States differ across income groups, and how might these differences affect long-term quality of life and wealth inequality trends?

## Significance

Our project bridges the gap between income inequality's present day impacts to future prospects through the lens of investment spending, consumer expenditures, and trends.

## Theoretical Framing

Our project views the differences in quality of life through the lens of data feminism, which claims that data is a non-neutral distributor of biases. We highlight the hidden narratives of the data we work with, and focus on both the surface and deeper insights on what the data indicates.
